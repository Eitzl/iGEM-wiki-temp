Safety

Our commitment to responsible research: lab safety, biosafety levels, GMO containment, and risk assessment for all project components.

Overview

Safety is foundational to iGEM and to responsible synthetic biology. This page documents our safety practices, risk assessments, and the measures taken to ensure our work poses no risk to team members, the public, or the environment.

Laboratory Safety

  • BSL-1 organisms: E. coli K12, Agrobacterium tumefaciens EHA105, Lemna minor — all BSL-1. No BSL-2 organisms used in primary experiments.
  • All team members completed institutional Biosafety Committee (IBC) training and lab safety orientation before beginning work.
  • PPE protocols: gloves, eye protection, and lab coat required at all times in the wet lab.
  • Chemical waste (EtBr, solvents) disposed per UCSC EH&S guidelines.
  • Autoclaving of all biological waste before disposal.

GMO Containment

All transgenic Lemna minor work is conducted in:

  • Physically isolated growth rooms with no drain connections to the environment
  • Closed growth vessels (sealed glass dishes or tissue culture flasks)
  • No outdoor or open-greenhouse work with transgenic lines
  • All transgenic material autoclaved before disposal

Melectin Safety Assessment

Melectin is an antimicrobial peptide. Relevant safety considerations:

  • Toxicity to humans: Low at research concentrations. Skin contact with dilute solutions not expected to cause harm. Standard PPE (gloves) sufficient.
  • Potential allergenicity: Bee venom peptides can cause allergic reactions in sensitized individuals. Team members with known bee venom allergies were assessed prior to work; epipen available in lab.
  • Environmental release: Melectin has no known persistence or bioaccumulation concerns at lab scale. Diluted in aqueous waste, treated per standard protein waste procedures.
