Environmental footprint of our production system compared to alternatives, and our commitment to sustainable lab practices.

Duckweed vs. SPPS: Environmental Comparison

Solid-phase peptide synthesis (SPPS), the current production method for Melectin, is chemically intensive: it requires large volumes of organic solvents (DMF, DCM, piperidine), resin, and coupling reagents. The waste stream from SPPS contains hazardous organics that require specialized disposal.

Our duckweed-based platform uses:

  • Water as the growth medium — no organic solvents
  • Sunlight or LED lighting as energy input (photosynthesis-based production)
  • CO₂ fixation — duckweed sequesters carbon during growth
  • Aqueous buffers for extraction — minimal organic solvent use
  • Duckweed biomass after extraction is compostable or can be fed to aquatic organisms
