Entrepreneurship

Market analysis, cost of goods modeling, IP landscape, and a business case for accessible Melectin production.

Market Problem

The global antimicrobial peptide market is projected to reach $[X] billion by 2030, driven by the antimicrobial resistance (AMR) crisis. Despite scientific promise, most AMPs remain inaccessible as research tools and are nowhere near clinical deployment due to production cost. Melectin specifically is sold at ~$400/mg by commercial suppliers — restricting research to well-funded institutions and making clinical-scale production economically impossible by conventional methods.

Value Proposition

Our plant-based, oil-body-purified production platform offers:

  • 10–100× cost reduction vs. SPPS (projected from yield and COGS modeling)
  • Scalability without bioreactor infrastructure — aquatic plant scale-up requires only larger vessel area
  • Self-purification reduces downstream processing cost to <2 steps vs. 5–8 for chromatography-based purification
  • Open-source potential: our protocols and parts are publicly available, enabling replication in resource-limited settings
