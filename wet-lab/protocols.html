Protocols

Step-by-step laboratory protocols for construct assembly, duckweed transformation, oil-body purification, and antimicrobial assays.

Protocol 1: Duckweed Maintenance Culture

Materials: Sterilized Hoagland's nutrient solution (half-strength), 16:8 h light:dark cycle, 25°C growth chamber, 500 mL glass dishes.

  1. Prepare half-strength Hoagland's solution (pH 5.8) in sterile glass bottles. Autoclave 121°C for 20 min.
  2. Surface-sterilize duckweed fronds: 70% EtOH 30 s → 10% bleach 2 min → 3× sterile dH₂O wash.
  3. Place ~20 fronds in 200 mL Hoagland's in glass dish. Cover with parafilm-pierced lid.
  4. Grow at 25°C under 16:8 h light:dark, ~80 µmol/m²/s cool white fluorescent.
  5. Subculture 1:5 every 5–7 days by transferring fronds to fresh media.

Protocol 2: Agrobacterium-Mediated Duckweed Transformation

Adapted from: [Cite source protocol — e.g., Yamamoto et al. or Stomp AM 2005]

  1. Grow Agrobacterium EHA105 (harboring binary vector) overnight in LB + rifampicin (25 µg/mL) + hygromycin (50 µg/mL) at 28°C, 200 rpm.
  2. Pellet cells at 3000 × g, 10 min. Resuspend in MS liquid medium + 200 µM acetosyringone to OD₆₀₀ = 0.5.
  3. Surface-sterilize healthy duckweed fronds (see Protocol 1 step 2). Pat dry on sterile filter paper.
  4. Immerse fronds in Agrobacterium suspension for 20 min at room temperature with gentle agitation.
  5. Blot excess suspension. Transfer fronds to co-cultivation medium (MS + 200 µM acetosyringone, 0.8% agar). Incubate 2 days in dark at 25°C.
  6. Transfer fronds to selection medium (MS agar + cefotaxime 500 µg/mL + hygromycin 25 µg/mL). Grow under 16:8 h light:dark.
  7. After 3–4 weeks, transfer hygromycin-resistant fronds (bright green, actively growing) to fresh selection medium.
  8. Confirm stable integration by genomic PCR using T-DNA-specific primers.

Protocol 3: Oil-Body Isolation

  1. Harvest 2–5 g fresh weight transgenic duckweed fronds. Flash-freeze in liquid nitrogen.
  2. Grind to fine powder in pre-chilled mortar and pestle. Transfer to 50 mL tube.
  3. Add 10 mL extraction buffer (50 mM Tris-HCl pH 8.0, 300 mM NaCl, 1 mM EDTA, 1× protease inhibitor cocktail). Vortex vigorously 2 min.
  4. Centrifuge at 1000 × g, 4°C, 10 min. Carefully remove floating oil-body layer with wide-bore pipette into a fresh tube.
  5. Wash oil bodies: resuspend in 10 mL wash buffer (same as extraction buffer + 0.3% Tween-20). Centrifuge 1000 × g, 10 min. Repeat 2×.
  6. Final wash with extraction buffer (no Tween-20).
  7. Resuspend oil bodies in 2 mL TEV cleavage buffer (50 mM Tris-HCl pH 8.0, 1 mM DTT, 0.5 mM EDTA).
  8. Add TEV protease (1:50 w/w ratio TEV:substrate). Incubate 16 h, 4°C, gentle rotation.
  9. Centrifuge 1000 × g, 10 min. Collect aqueous phase containing released Melectin.
  10. Quantify by BCA protein assay. Confirm by anti-Melectin Western blot or LC-MS/MS.

Protocol 4: Minimum Inhibitory Concentration (MIC) Assay

Standard: CLSI M07-Ed11 broth microdilution.

  1. Prepare 2-fold serial dilutions of Melectin in 96-well plate (columns 1–10): range 0.5–256 µg/mL in CAMHB (cation-adjusted Mueller-Hinton broth).
  2. Column 11: growth control (broth + bacteria, no peptide). Column 12: sterility control (broth only).
  3. Inoculate each well with test organism at 5 × 10⁵ CFU/mL final concentration.
  4. Incubate 16–20 h at 37°C (bacteria) or 30°C (if using non-standard organisms).
  5. Read OD₆₀₀ on plate reader. Add resazurin (10 µL of 0.015% solution) to each well; incubate 2 h. Read fluorescence (excitation 560 nm / emission 590 nm) to confirm MIC visually.
  6. MIC = lowest concentration with no visible growth / no color change.

Protocol 5: Hemolysis Assay

  1. Collect fresh sheep or human erythrocytes (ethics-approved). Wash 3× in PBS at 500 × g, 5 min.
  2. Prepare 2% RBC suspension in PBS.
  3. Set up 2-fold serial dilutions of Melectin (0.5–256 µg/mL) in PBS in 96-well plate.
  4. Add equal volume 2% RBC suspension to each well. Mix gently.
  5. Incubate 1 h at 37°C without shaking.
  6. Centrifuge 500 × g, 5 min. Transfer 100 µL supernatant to fresh plate.
  7. Read OD₄₁₅ (hemoglobin absorbance). 100% hemolysis control: 1% Triton X-100. 0% control: PBS only.
  8. HC₅₀ = concentration causing 50% hemolysis. Calculate % hemolysis = (OD sample − OD₀%) / (OD₁₀₀% − OD₀%) × 100.
