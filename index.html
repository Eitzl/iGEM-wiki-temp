UC Santa Cruz — iGEM 2026

Accessible Melectin via Plant-Based Biosynthesis

We're engineering duckweed to produce Melectin — a potent antimicrobial peptide from bee venom — using an oleosin oil-body fusion strategy for low-cost, scalable purification. Targeting Lyme disease, Parkinson's, and cancer.

Learn More Meet the Team

The Challenge

Why Melectin?

Melectin is a promising therapeutic peptide — but its source (bee venom collection) makes it expensive and inaccessible. We're changing that.

🦠

Antimicrobial Action

Melectin disrupts bacterial membranes via its cationic amphipathic helical structure, showing activity against Lyme-associated Borrelia and drug-resistant pathogens.

Mechanism →
🌿

Duckweed Expression

We use Lemna minor as our chassis — fast-growing, aquatic, and amenable to stable transformation — to produce Melectin at scale without fermenters.

Expression system →
💧

Oil-Body Purification

By fusing Melectin to oleosin, the peptide rides self-assembling oil bodies that float out of cell lysate — eliminating costly chromatography steps.

Purification strategy →
🧬

Therapeutic Targets

Melectin's unique properties position it against Lyme disease, Parkinson's (amyloid disruption), and cancer cell lines — a multi-indication pipeline.
